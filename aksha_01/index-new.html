Opinion

The status of India's national health presents many reasons to be optimistic. Results from the last National Family Health Survey 2015 - 2016 (NFHS-4) has shown declining trends in key health indicators like Infant Mortality Rate (IMR) and Under-five Mortality Rate (U5MR). The government is also making a concerted effort towards bridging the healthcare access gap by focusing on low-income and marginalized communities across the country, previously unreached by quality healthcare services.

Technology-based interventions that enable real-time access and allow for ‘here-and-now’ review of the impact of health programs, are being deployed and are expected to be game-changing. Further, the high priority given to building an improved sanitation eco-system – a prerequisite for a healthy India - is underway.

But much more needs to be done if ambitious national health outcomes are to be realized. These include, amongst others, enabling good health and nutrition for children to ensure they reach their full intellectual capability as well as focusing on eliminating diseases that disproportionately affect the poor such as tuberculosis and lymphatic filariasis.

What will it take to get there? Where will the resources come from? And which are most the effective ways to improve health and reduce poverty?
