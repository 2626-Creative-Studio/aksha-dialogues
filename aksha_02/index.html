IN THE SPOTLIGHT

India has made sustained progress in the battle against TB and Multi-drug resistant TB (MDR TB). In 2012, TB notification was made mandatory and till date, nearly 20 million patients have been treated under The Revised National TB Control Programme (RNTCP) – the country’s flagship program to tackle TB. Incidence rates were reduced from 289 per lakh per year in 2000 to 217 per lakh per year by 2015 and the mortality due to TB has reduced from 56 per lac per year in 2000 to 36 per lac per year in 2015.

India is also leveraging technology in the fight against TB. The RNTCP uses the NIKSHAY platform - an ICT enabled state-of-art surveillance system which is set up to get notification of TB cases at diagnosis. This web enabled and case based monitoring application tracks cases reported from both public and private sector.

However, despite this progress, India still accounts for one-fourth of the global TB burden and is also among the ten countries with the largest gaps between notifications of new and relapse (incident) of TB cases. Timely detection, particularly from the private sector where most of the patients first seek care, continues to pose a challenge. Of the 40% of TB cases are catered to by the private sector, data shows that nearly half of them are still missed by the TB notification system. Further gaps in tackling TB/MDR/RR-TB as well as HIV associated TB also remain significant.

For India to eradicate TB by 2025, a continuous, sustained and collaborative effort is required. Further, gaps in diagnosis and treatment interventions, as well as leveraging of technology to tackle this challenge must be made a priority.

FROM THE JOURNALS

Here are two interesting studies focusing on some of the main challenges in tackling TB: how to detect MDR-TB early and deploy technology to increase adherence of TB medication.

A demographic dividend of the FP2020 Initiative and the SDG reproductive health target: Case studies of India and Nigeria

The demographic dividend, defined as the economic growth potential resulting from favourable shifts in population age structure following rapid fertility decline, has been widely employed to advocate improving access to family planning. The current framework focuses on the long-term potential, while the short-term benefits may also help persuade policy makers to invest in family planning.

Use of Mobile Technology by Community-Based Health Workers to Promote Maternal and Child Health in Bihar, India (ICT-CCS)

This study is designed to evaluate the impact of use of mobile technology by community-based health workers on health-promoting behaviours among women related to reproductive, maternal, newborn and child health and nutrition in Bihar, India.
